All About Astrology

Introduction

Astrology has been around for thousands of years, but it's still very much a topic of interest. It's not just about getting lucky and making money; there are many interesting aspects to the practice and science behind it. In this article, we'll take an in-depth look at what astrology is, what it can do for you, how you can use it to your advantage—and why you should stay current with trends as well.

The Zodiac

The Zodiac is a band of sky that is divided into 12 equal sections, each 30 degrees wide. It's also called the ecliptic and it's actually a circle with the earth at its center. The zodiac is divided into 12 equal sections, each 30 degrees wide -- one for each month in an entire year (though during certain times of year there are more days than usual). So when we say "the sun was in Gemini" or "the moon was in Aries," we're really saying something like "the sun was rising at sunrise on March 20th." There are also other things in the sky, like constellations and planets. Constellations are groups of stars that form an imaginary picture (like the Big Dipper), while planets are just like the moon -- they shine by reflecting light from the sun.

Sun Signs

The Sun is the source of all life on Earth. It's also the center of our solar system, and determines what kind of day you're going to have. There are two main types of astrological signs: fire signs and water signs. Each sign belongs to either one or the other type—for example, Scorpio belongs to both a water sign (Scorpio) and a fire sign (Sagittarius). The Sun moves through each sign during its 12-year cycle before moving on again to another one in its cycle—and since your birthday falls within this cycle, it will fall in one particular sign according to how well you fit that particular combination!

Moon Signs

The moon signs are the same as the sun signs. They're also called rising signs, because they rise after the sun has set. Moon signs are good indicators of your personality and how you're likely to behave in different situations. If someone is a Cancer or Leo, for example, it's likely that they will be more emotional than someone who was born under Virgo or Scorpio (because these two signs have a strong connection with water). Your Moon and Rising Signs

Your Moon and Rising Signs Your moon sign is the sign that your moon was in at the time of your birth. Your rising sign is the one that was rising above the eastern horizon at the time of your birth. This information can help you understand how people react to certain situations, but it's also important because it influences your personality, which has a huge impact on how others perceive you as an individual. Astrologists believe that people's personalities are influenced by their birth charts and thus, they believe that their moon and rising signs determine emotional characteristics such as who we are romantically attracted toward or what kind of career path we choose for ourselves (like being an artist versus being a doctor).

The Planets

The planets are the bodies that orbit the sun. There are eight planets in our solar system, with a ninth one being Pluto. Each planet has its own name and characteristics: Mercury is closest to Earth, followed by Venus; then comes Mars; then Jupiter; Saturn; Uranus and Neptune. The names of these planets were derived from Roman gods or mythological figures who were believed to have had some influence over humans' lives on earth (i.e., Mercury represents quickness). In addition to their namesakes being associated with specific traits or abilities within each religion's pantheon of deities (Mercury for quickness), each planet also has an element associated with it: Mercury has air because it is closest along with Venus which means water because it's between her sisters Earth & Mars...etc.. The planets are also associated with the four elements in Greek mythology: air, earth, fire and water. Astrology is interesting, but it is important to stay on top of trends as well.

You might have heard that astrology is “not a science,” and this is true. It's not any kind of science at all—it's just a reflection of the human condition. Astrology isn't predictive in any way; it doesn't tell you what will happen to you or how to make decisions about your life based on the planets' positions on their orbits around the Sun. But that doesn't mean you shouldn't pay attention to trends! Trends are important because they help us understand how things work in our lives and society as a whole (for example: why we wear jeans today vs yesterday). So if there are trends happening with your sign (sun sign), then stay informed about these things so that when one does come up again later down the line (like an eclipse) it will make sense for everyone else who has been following along too since birth!

Conclusion
